# Why printable-sleeve exists

Sexual wellness products are a $1.5 billion market growing at 9.5% annually.
Yet for millions of people, buying one is still out of reach — not because
the products don't exist, but because of price, stigma, and geography.

This project exists to change that.

---

## The three barriers we eliminate

### 1. Cost

A commercial masturbation sleeve costs €60–90.
The materials to print one cost €2–5.

That gap exists not because of engineering complexity, but because of
marketing, distribution, retail margins, and packaging. None of those
add any functional value for the person using it.

FDM printing eliminates all of them.

### 2. Stigma

Studies show that approximately 29% of people who want a sexual wellness
product never buy one — not because of price, but because of stigma.

The act of buying one is itself a barrier:
walking into a shop, receiving a package, seeing a charge on a bank statement.

A file you download and print at home leaves no trace.
That is not a workaround. That is a genuine solution to a real problem.

### 3. Access

A person in a rural area, a country with restrictive import laws,
or simply without disposable income has no realistic access to this
product category at commercial prices.

A 3D printer and a spool of TPU changes that.
The design travels as a file. Manufacturing happens locally, on demand.

## The health case

This is not just about convenience or cost. There is a documented
public health argument.

A study published in European Urology (Rider et al., 2016, based on
Harvard/Health Professionals Follow-up data) found that men who
ejaculated 21 or more times per month had a 20% lower risk of
prostate cancer compared to those who ejaculated 4–7 times per month.

Separate research consistently links sexual activity and orgasm with
reduced cortisol levels, improved sleep quality, and lower cardiovascular
stress markers.

Access to appropriate tools matters for these outcomes.
A product that is unaffordable or inaccessible is not a solution.

## Why open source

Commercial products in this category are closed, proprietary, and
designed to be replaced rather than repaired or customised.

Open source means:
- Anyone can adapt the design for their body
- Anyone can improve it and share the improvement
- No company can restrict access or raise prices
- The design survives its creator

We use CC BY-SA 4.0. You can use this design, modify it, and even
sell prints of it — as long as you keep it open under the same terms.

---

## Why now

Two things made this possible:

**Bambu Lab (2022–2024)** democratised FDM printing. A machine that
produces reliable, accurate prints at 15 mm/s in TPU now costs under €200.
That did not exist five years ago.

**OpenSCAD + BOSL2** makes the design fully parametric. One number —
your girth divided by 3.14 — adapts the entire geometry to your body.
No CAD skills required.

The technology was always theoretically possible. Now it is practical.

---

## What this is not

This is not a replacement for human connection.
This is not a statement about relationships or sexuality.
This is a functional object, designed to be printed, used, and improved.

The same argument that applies here applies to any product where:
- There is a large gap between manufacturing cost and retail price
- Stigma reduces access for people who would benefit
- The underlying technology is now accessible to individuals

Sexual wellness happens to be a particularly clear example.
But the principle is general.
